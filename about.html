About Me

I am an energy engineer from Spain, with deep computer science skills, such as programming, scripting and open-source development. I started my professional career in HEXCEL, optimizing one of its composite material manufacturing processes, the measures proposed reduced more than a 50 % the primary energy consumption of the plant. Then, focusing my career on the concept of Industry 4.0, I worked in an additive manufacturing start-up, but after completing my Msc, I found my passion in simulation and programming. For this reason, I traveled to France to work in one of the most prestigious laboratories in computational fluid dynamics, CORIA, developing in OpenFOAM (C++) a dynamic rotating mesh solver for turbines able to compute a variable angular speed depending on the moment generated in the blades. I returned to Spain to work as simulation engineer and business developer for an ESCO, carrying out numerous resource and financial simulations for large self-consumption photovoltaic plants, as well as dynamic energy simulation of buildings. Nowadays, after being accepted in the Industrial Doctorant program of the Community of Madrid, I carry out my studies as doctor in CONTACTICA and IMDEA ENERGY, developing a new eco-design software for sustainability-oriented optimisation of industrial processes based on a novel methodology which embeds modelling, process simulation, life cycle sustainability assessment and multi-objective optimization algorithms.
